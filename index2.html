( slow erosion )

slow erosion is the collaboration between Hannah Simmons and Sarah Fetterman.

They have developed a shared practice utilizing movement, sculpture, and technology through which they explore intangible and ephemeral states of being. Together, they develop a logical framework to unearth and preserve those things that are slippery, elusive; those things that are hard to hold, hard to name, harder yet to understand. Their process is careful and intricate, approaching these liminal states with a reverence, logic, and curiosity. They work to translate the ephemeral into artifact; to slow the process of disappearance; to keep a record of the residues that we leave. They try to understand: What is the substance of memory? Why is grief sinuous? What is the texture of a ghost? How heavy is loneliness?

While conceptually, their work considers the same questions, they approach making from opposite perspectives. Simmons’ cerebral and algorithmic approach meets Fetterman’s material-oriented thought process. Their work grows through the conversation between materials and metaphysics. Living somewhere in between genres, they make work that is part dance, part sculpture, part interactive installation. Together, they create visually and intellectually striking works that tug at their viewers with a sort of nostalgia. Not a nostalgia for the past but, instead, for something that sidesteps time; something deeply ingrained within each of us, that has been felt but left unnamed.

The Seattle-based duo makes genre-defying installation based work.

They both graduated from Bennington College, then rediscovered one another in Seattle where they began collaborating in early 2016. Their work has been shown at SOIL Gallery, Hybrid Architecture, and the Center on Contemporary Art. Their projects have been partially funded by the GAP Award (2017) and the CityArtist Grant (2018).

Sarah Fetterman

Welder, Woodworker, & Visual Thinker

Sarah's performative sculpture work has been shown at Seattle Center and she has an upcoming Visiting Artist position at Bennington College culminating in a show at MASS MoCA in 2020. She has attended Residencies at Franconia Sculpture Park, Vermont Studio Center, and Xochi Quetzal. She is the Studio Manager for Ginny Ruffner, where she recently managed and installed Ruffner’s show at the Renwick Gallery of the Smithsonian. Previously, Sarah was a Project Lead and Fabricator for sculptor John Grade for four years including managing a large-scale installation that is installed in Arte Sella Sculpture Park in Northern Italy.

Hannah Simmons

Performer, Digital Artist, & Mathematician

Hannah received her degree in mathematics and dance. Her own choreographic work has been supported by Velocity’s Bridge Project, Arts Incubator at Cornish Playhouse, and Artist Trust. During her time at Bennington, she had the privilege of spending several months touring with The Forsythe Company (Frankfurt, Germany) through an educational exchange. She has performed with José Mateo Ballet Theatre (Boston, MA), Verlaine & McCann Present, House of Verlaine, Daniel Roberts (Columbus, OH), Coriolis Dance, Beth Terwilliger/The Gray, and Julie Tobiason, among others.
